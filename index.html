Ulam spiral

An Ulam spiral is a way to order the natural numbers numbers which visually demonstrates areas with a high density of primes. Prime numbers are demarcated by red cells in the grid below.

"Wait! Don't prime numbers famously elude capture by neat mathematical formulae? Why should any particular layout of the number system generate the appearance of order amongst the primes?" I hear you cry. Good question! Whilst some areas of high prime density is expected (more here), the extent of the patterns you are seeing with your very own eyes in the image below represent regularity that no current mathematical formula has yet fully captured.
